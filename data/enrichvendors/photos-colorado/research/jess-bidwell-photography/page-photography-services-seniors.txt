The goal isn't perfection &mdash; it's feeling comfortable and confident. 
I recommend starting with neutral, earthy tones that photograph beautifully in natural landscapes, and building outfits that feel true to you. 
You're always welcome to bring multiple outfits, and I'm happy to help you plan everything so it feels cohesive without being overdone.

A Few Things You Might Be Wondering 
What if I feel awkward? 
You're not alone &mdash; almost everyone says this before their session. 
You don't need to know how to pose or what to do. I guide you naturally throughout the session so nothing feels forced or uncomfortable. Most seniors end up saying it felt way easier (and more fun) than they expected. 
​ 
Can I bring multiple outfits? 
Yes, absolutely. 
Most seniors bring 2&ndash;3 outfits to add variety to their gallery. We can plan ahead so everything works well together and fits the locations we choose. The goal is to give you options that feel like you, not overdone. 
​ 
Can parents come? 
Of course. 
Parents are always welcome &mdash; whether that's helping with outfits, giving input, or just being there for the experience. I'm happy to include them in a few photos at the end as well if you'd like. 
​ ​ ​ 
Can we choose a unique location? 
Definitely &mdash; I actually encourage it. 
If there's a place that feels meaningful to you or fits your personality, I'd love to incorporate it. I'm also happy to suggest locations around Telluride, Ouray, Ridgway, and Montrose that match the look and feel you're going for. 
​ ​ ​ 
Can I bring props or personal items? 
Yes &mdash; when they feel like an extension of you. 
Whether it's something tied to your interests or just part of your everyday style, I encourage keeping things simple and intentional so your photos still feel natural and timeless. 
Somewhere between who you've been and who you're becoming &mdash; let's capture it.
