Telluride Wedding Photography &#038; Film
 Telluride. Mountain Village. The San Juans.
Telluride sits in a box canyon at 8,750 feet, surrounded by 14,000-foot peaks on three sides. The terrain is unlike anywhere else in Colorado — and it shapes every wedding held here. Ceremonies at Mountain Lodge Telluride and San Sophia Overlook, tucked at 10,540 feet above Mountain Village and accessed by gondola, produce some of the most dramatic ceremony imagery in North America. Further into the San Juans, Dunton Hot Springs — a restored ghost town deep in the Dolores River Valley — offers a setting so remote and deliberate that it demands a different kind of coverage entirely. We&#8217;ve documented Telluride weddings across each of these venues and throughout the surrounding region, including Ouray, Ridgway, Silverton, and Durango. The San Juan Mountains reward photographers who understand how to work with rapidly shifting alpine light, high-elevation weather, and landscapes that frame every moment without needing to be managed. Telluride Weddings
 Questions About Telluride Wedding Photography 
What makes Telluride a unique wedding destination? Telluride occupies a box canyon in the San Juan Mountains at nearly 9,000 feet of elevation, surrounded by 13,000-foot peaks on three sides. There is no equivalent setting in Colorado — the scale is extraordinary, the light is theatrical, and the sense of genuine remoteness is real. Unlike Vail or Aspen, Telluride does not feel like a resort town that has been built around wedding infrastructure. It feels like a place that couples choose for what it is, not what it offers as a venue. We have documented weddings at Mountain Lodge Telluride and across the broader Mountain Village, and the setting consistently produces some of our most compelling work.
 When is the best time of year to get married in Telluride? Late September is Telluride&#8217;s most visually dramatic season — aspen color in the San Juan Mountains peaks with an intensity that surpasses anywhere else in Colorado, and the canyon walls turn gold in a way that is genuinely extraordinary to photograph. July and August offer the most stable outdoor ceremony conditions at elevation. Winter weddings in Telluride are rare and intentional — the town&#8217;s remote access via Montrose Regional Airport and challenging mountain road conditions make logistics demanding, but the visual payoff under snow against the box canyon walls is unlike anything else in the state.
 How do guests get to Telluride for a destination wedding? Most destination wedding guests fly into Montrose Regional Airport (MTJ), approximately an hour from Telluride, or Denver International Airport (DEN), approximately five to six hours by car. Telluride Regional Airport (TEX) handles direct private and charter flights and is the most convenient option for guests traveling from major metropolitan areas. Shuttle services between Montrose and Telluride are well established. For multi-day destination weddings, coordinating guest accommodation blocks at properties like Mountain Lodge Telluride simplifies logistics significantly.
 What venues are available for weddings in Telluride? The primary dedicated wedding venues in the Telluride area are Mountain Lodge Telluride in Mountain Village and the Madeline Hotel (now operating under the Auberge brand). Both are resort properties with on-site accommodations that support full destination weekend formats. The town of Telluride itself — the historic main street, Riverside Park, and the surrounding canyon floor — is also used for ceremonies and elopements. Our Mountain Lodge Telluride venue guide covers the property in detail, and our full Colorado venues guide places Telluride within the broader landscape of Southwest Colorado wedding options.
 How far in advance should I book a Telluride wedding photographer? For September weekends — the most sought-after photography conditions in the San Juan Mountains — booking 12 to 18 months in advance is recommended. Telluride&#8217;s wedding market is smaller and more selective than Vail or Aspen, which means the best dates at the best venues book quickly among a smaller pool of photographers and planners. We take on a limited number of Telluride weddings each year. If you have a date in mind, reach out through our booking page to check availability and begin the conversation.
