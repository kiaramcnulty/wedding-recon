Hiring your dream photographer is just one piece of the puzzle for your elopement or wedding. 
I have couples that decide to do a totally DIY elopement, which is so badass and something I just wouldn’t have the patience for. The majority of my couples are hiring at least two to three other vendors to create their perfect Colorado wedding day.
Sure, You could use Zola, or Equally Wed to find &#8220;LGBTQ+ Friendly&#8221; wedding vendors &#8211; but not all the amazing allied vendors can afford to be listed on these websites. I decided to do the hard work of putting together a list of allied wedding vendors that you won&#8217;t have to awkwardly ask, &#8220;Will you respect me and my partner on our wedding day?&#8221;
If you don’t know, I hate the gatekeepy-ness of the wedding industry. It’s not a competition, and every couple deserves the information they need to make the best decision for them. I also know it can be hard to figure out if a vendor’s values align with your own. The amount of shady shit I see on Threads or in social media groups is jaw dropping. I only promote alternative wedding vendors who believe: 
Marriage Equality 
Black Lives Matter 
Science is real 
Trans Lives Matter 
Women’s rights are human rights 
As you’re planning your elopement or wedding, check out this list of a few of my favorite allied wedding vendors in Colorado! 
Must Knows 
Repeat after me. We do NOT negotiate with vendors. This is not Facebook Marketplace. Be open and honest about your budget, and vendors will always be happy to see what they can provide for that price point. 
Don’t wait too long to book! Just like photographers, certain vendors can book up fast. Once you have a date and venue solidified, it’s time to start hiring the vendors you want. 
When you hire me, I become your photographer AND planner (if you&#8217;re eloping). I’m here to help coordinate with your allied wedding vendors in Colorado and even pick things up for you, like desserts or flowers.

Florists 
These Colorado business owners create some of the most beautiful and unique floral arrangements. If there&#8217;s one thing you need to splurge on, it&#8217;s flowers. 
Please Please Please don&#8217;t DIY your flowers. Please. 
We also don&#8217;t buy wooden flowers &#8211; I know you have been getting those ads, but please, for the sake of all things good, avoid. Dried Florals are always a great alternative to real and will last forever! Most floral artists offer the option of Dried florals, don&#8217;t be afraid to ask. From bouquets to suit accents, they’ve got you covered. On average, Florists book one year in advance.
