How Do You Choose the Right Elopement Package?
Choose based on how long you want the day to be, not just the number of photos. A four-hour elopement is right for couples who want a focused ceremony experience at one location. An eight to ten-hour day is for couples who want to move around, hit multiple spots, add activities, and make the whole day an adventure. The photo count follows the time. Longer days produce more images because more things happen.

Elopement Package FAQ
What are Colorado elopement packages? Vows and Peaks elopement packages bundle photography with location planning, permit coordination, and timeline management. My packages are designed to simplify mountain elopement planning by combining professional photography with full planning support in a single service.
What is included in a Colorado elopement package with Vows and Peaks? Every package includes photography, permit research, a custom location plan, a day-of timeline, and access to trusted vendors. The all-inclusive adds video, hair, makeup, and florals.
How much does a Colorado elopement cost? Vows and Peaks packages range from $4,700 to $11,500. A traditional Colorado wedding averages around $36,000. Planning is included in all elopement packages.
How far in advance should we book a Colorado elopement package? Most couples book 6-12 months out to get first choice of location, date, and vendors. I regularly work with 2-3 month timelines too, especially in shoulder season. If you&#8217;re dreaming of Telluride in peak wildflower season (mid-July through early August), book early. Permits and vendor availability go fast.
Can family and friends come to our elopement? Absolutely. More than half my elopements include family or a small group. The Alpine package is specifically built for micro weddings with a crew. How many photos will we get? About 300 edited images for 4-hour coverage. The 8-hour Wilderness package delivers 325-425. The 10-hour Alpine package delivers 375 or more. Every image is fully edited and high-resolution, delivered in a downloadable gallery.
Can we customize our package or add extra hours? Yes. Couples can add extra photography hours, additional vendors, or extended coverage for more locations, adventure activities, or celebrations. Packages are designed to be flexible based on weather, season, and preferences.
Do you work weekends? I do but I try and guide couples to doing weekends. Trailheads are less crowded, vendors are more available, permits are easier to secure, and you have the mountains to yourselves. The experience is measurably better.
What are some considerations for a mountain elopement? Mountain elopements in Colorado require planning for altitude, weather, and terrain. Arrive a few days early to acclimate and stay hydrated. Morning ceremonies help avoid afternoon storms, and layers plus sturdy hiking boots are essential.

What happens if the weather is bad on our elopement day? Every elopement I do has a weather backup plan built in. Colorado weather moves fast. Some of those days produced the best photos.

Can we legally get married in Colorado without an officiant? Yes. Colorado is one of only a handful of states that allows self-solemnization, meaning you and your partner can legally marry each other without an ordained officiant present. You will still need to obtain a Colorado marriage license from the county clerk&#8217;s office in the county where you plan to marry, and both of you will sign the license as co-officiants.
