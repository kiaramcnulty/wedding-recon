What is self-solemnization in Colorado?
Self-solemnization in Colorado means you and your partner can legally marry each other without an officiant, a judge, or any witnesses. You simply obtain a marriage license, exchange vows in any location you choose, and sign the license yourselves as “the parties to the marriage.” It’s legally binding and recognized everywhere. 
Colorado is one of only two locations in the U.S. where self-solemnization comes with zero conditions. The other is Washington D.C. Several other states allow it, including California, Pennsylvania, Kansas, Montana, Illinois, Nevada, Maine, and Wisconsin, but Nevada, Maine, and Wisconsin require a religious exemption. Colorado does not. You can be spiritual, atheist, or anything in between and this is still fully legal!
This isn’t a loophole, it’s a deeply rooted legal tradition! Colorado has recognized self-solemnization since before statehood, and it remains one of only a handful of states that offer this freedom without any additional requirements. If you’ve ever dreamed of a private hiking elopement on a remote peak or a quiet moment at an alpine lake with not a single other soul around, Colorado’s marriage laws were practically written for you!
How do you self-solemnize in Colorado?
To self-solemnize in Colorado, schedule an appointment at any county clerk’s office, bring valid ID and $30, get your license, have your ceremony within 35 days, sign the license as “the parties to the marriage,” and return it within 63 days to the same office. The whole process is super easy and simple. 
Here’s the full step-by-step breakdown: 
Step 1 
Locate the closest county :
Your license can come from any Colorado county clerk regardless of where your ceremony happens. I suggest picking the one nearest your elopement location and checking their appointment availability before locking in a date. Most counties now use an appointment system, and some book out a few weeks during peak summer season.
Step 2 
Schedule Your Appointment: 
If you are traveling from outside of the state, you can schedule this for the same day as your marriage, but I would advise against that. Most counties let you schedule your appointment online before you arrive. Things come up and you don&#8217;t want a paperwork delay eating into your elopement day!
Step 3 
Pick Up Your Colorado Marriage License: 
Don&#8217;t forget your essentials: Bring your ID to the clerks office, and $30 to seal the deal. With all that in hand, you&#8217;re just one step away from making your marriage a reality. Let&#8217;s do this thing!
Step 4 
Self Marry In Colorado: 
Before you leave Colorado, make sure to drop off your signed license or mail it back to the county clerk within 63 days. And yes, you can get your marriage license and marry the same day in Colorado if your timeline is tight. There is no mandatory waiting period.

Self-solemnizing FAQ 
Do you need an officiant to elope in Colorado? In Colorado you do not need an officiant to elope. It’s one of the few states that allow self-solemnization, which means you and your partner can legally marry each other without a third party. To understand exactly how this works, check out my guide to self-solemnizing your Colorado elopement. Many couples also wonder if they can get their marriage license and marry the same day in Colorado, and the answer is yes.
Can we still have an officiant if we want to? Yes. Self-solemnization doesn&#8217;t mean you can&#8217;t have an officiant. It means you don&#8217;t have to. You can have a friend lead the ceremony, invite a family member to say a few words, or blend a traditional script with your own vows. There are no rules.
What happens if we don’t return the license within 63 days? Contact the county clerk&#8217;s office immediately. The process for what happens next varies by county. In most cases, you will need to obtain a new license and re-sign. This is rare but it does happen, usually when couples forget to mail it back after returning home. Mail it before you leave Colorado if you can.
How do we ensure our self-solemnizing elopement is legally recognized? Follow the four steps above: obtain your marriage license from the closest county clerk, bring your ID and $30, sign the license at your ceremony, and return it to the county clerk within 63 days. That&#8217;s it. No additional filings, no witnesses, nothing else required.
What elements should we include in our self-solemnizing ceremony? There are no legal requirements for what your ceremony has to include. Most couples exchange vows and rings, but the format is completely up to you. I&#8217;ve captured everything from two-minute sunrise ceremonies to full productions with readings, music, and champagne. Some couples do handfasting, some write elaborate scripts, some just say what comes naturally in the moment. All of it counts.
Can a friend marry us? Yes. In Colorado your friend doesn&#8217;t need to be ordained or certified to lead your ceremony. They can write a script, say a few words, or just stand there and cheer you on. The only legal requirement is that you and your partner sign the marriage license yourselves. Your friend&#8217;s involvement is purely ceremonial.
Can we still have a photographer or guests at a Self-Solemnized Elopement? Yes to both. Self-solemnization has no witness requirement in Colorado, but you&#8217;re absolutely free to invite guests, bring a photographer, or have your whole friend group there if that&#8217;s what you want. The only people who need to sign the license are the two of you.
Is officiating your own marriage the same thing as marrying yourselves in Colorado? Yes. Officiating your own marriage, self-solemnizing, and self-uniting are all terms for the same thing in Colorado. You perform your own ceremony, you sign your own license, and the state recognizes it exactly like any other legal marriage.
Where do we get our marriage license for our self solemnization? From the county clerk&#8217;s office in whichever Colorado county is closest to where you&#8217;re getting married. I recommend scheduling your appointment a few days before your ceremony rather than the same day, just to give yourself a buffer if anything comes up. Out of state couples can often have the license mailed to them, and Colorado locals can do a virtual appointment.
What Is the Difference Between a Self-Solemnized Elopement and a Traditional Elopement? A traditional elopement still requires a licensed officiant to sign your marriage license. A self-solemnized elopement does not. In Colorado, self-solemnization is about as close to the original spirit of eloping as you can get. Just the two of you, wherever you want, doing it entirely on your own terms.
Who can sign our marriage license? You and your partner are the only required signatures on a Colorado self-solemnization marriage license. That said, you can invite friends or family to sign as well if you want. There&#8217;s no limit, and I&#8217;ve seen couples fill both sides of the license at a celebration after the ceremony.
Is self-solemnization in Colorado actually legal? Yes. Colorado law (C.R.S. 14-2-109) explicitly allows couples to solemnize their own marriage without an officiant or witnesses. Your self-solemnized marriage carries the same legal weight as any traditional wedding performed by a judge or religious officiant.
How much does a Colorado marriage license cost? A Colorado marriage license costs $30. That&#8217;s the only required fee for a self-solemnization. There is no officiant fee, no witness fee, and no ceremony permit required for most locations.
What do you actually say during a self-solemnization ceremony? There is no required script for a self-solemnization ceremony in Colorado. Most couples I work with exchange personal vows and rings. Some add readings, music, handfasting, or a champagne toast. You can say as much or as little as you want.
Can our dog sign the marriage license? Yes, but not all Colorado counties still accept pet signatures on marriage licenses. As of late 2024, Denver, Arapahoe, and El Paso County (Colorado Springs) have stopped accepting paw prints. Call your specific county clerk before your appointment and ask. If they say yes, bring non-toxic ink and keep the paw print inside the signature box. Most of the San Juan area counties (Ouray, San Miguel, San Juan) still accept them. Call ahead.
Can we get our marriage license and get married the same day? Yes. Colorado has no waiting period between obtaining your marriage license and using it. You can pick it up in the morning and get married that afternoon if you want to.
Is self-solemnization recognized in other states if we move? Yes. Once your Colorado marriage license is filed with the county clerk, your marriage is legally recognized in all 50 states. You do not need to re-register or redo anything when you return home.
Do we need to be Colorado residents to self-solemnize? No. Colorado marriage licenses are available to anyone, regardless of where you live. A lot of couples fly in specifically because Colorado is one of the only places where self-solemnization comes with no conditions attached. Out-of-state couples can often pre-apply for their license online before arriving, and the signed license can typically be mailed back to the county clerk after the wedding.
Is a self-solemnized marriage valid for name changes and insurance? Yes. A self-solemnized marriage in Colorado is a legally recognized marriage throughout the United States. You’ll receive a certified marriage certificate after the license is recorded, which you can use for name changes, insurance benefits, tax status updates, and all other purposes.
Can we self-solemnize in a National Park or on public land? Yes. The marriage license does not restrict where your ceremony takes place. However, some National Parks and National Forests require a permit for any ceremony or gathering in specific zones, regardless of size. On BLM land and most wilderness areas in the San Juans, a private ceremony between two people with a photographer typically does not require a permit. Check with the specific land management agency for your exact location before your date.
