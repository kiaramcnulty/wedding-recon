 Piper and Stephen&#8217;s Crooked Willow Farms wedding 
Posted By : Carl Bower / 
0 comments / 
Under : Uncategorized 
When Piper told me she was having her wedding at Crooked Willow Farms, I drew a blank. Now it&#8217;s one of my favorite venues. The venue is in the rolling hills of Larkspur, which happens to almost exactly between Colorado Springs and Denver, convenient to both but visually far removed. There is an actual farm on the edge of the site, but it’s a purpose-built space that mostly feels rustic with stables, a barn, etc. but without the compromises of places just tried to capitalize on the wedding business. The space feels open and grounded at the same time—weathered wood, old brick, enough distance from urban areas to allow a psychological shift. This day leaned made the most of that space, with a couple who were far more interested in being present than performing for the camera.

 Teresa and Luke&#8217;s Devil&#8217;s Thumb Ranch Wedding 
Posted By : Carl Bower / 
0 comments / 
Under : Uncategorized 
I hadn&#8217;t covered a Devil&#8217;s Thumb Ranch wedding before, but as a Denver wedding photographer contemplating a 12-hour day in the August heat, spending the weekend in much-cooler Winter Park was more than welcome. The ranch is a resort just a few miles north of town tucked into its own little valley. That location is important for day or night it feels much further from the closest town than it actually is and there&#8217;s no visual evidence that there&#8217;s anyone there but you. The landscape is gorgeous and undisturbed.  There are cabins, lodges, restaurants and a number of different event venues, but it&#8217;s all rather quiet and disguised. It&#8217;s one of the nicer venues I&#8217;ve been to in Colorado.

 Maria and Ilya&#8217;s Black Cat Farm Wedding 
Posted By : Carl Bower / 
0 comments / 
Under : Uncategorized 
Maria and Ilya were looking for a Denver wedding photographer who understood their Manhattan vibe. I&#8217;ve lived in New York and they&#8217;re recent transplants who brought a small chunk of the city out to Longmont for their wedding at Black Cat Farm . It&#8217;s sort of a gourmet farm to table place that brought the tables to the farm itself. Most of what they serve comes from the same property, and I saw them picking the greens and vegetables that would be served that evening. I don&#8217;t recall what they served as animal protein, but they were doubtlessly discrete on that front. In any case, the venue is surrounded by rolling hills and feels very removed from everything.
As a Denver wedding photographer, I&#8217;ve worked in a lot of places up in the mountains or these rural venues sprinkled along the front range. Just being outside the city or the suburbs, driving down gravel roads through canyons or farmland to the ceremony site puts one in a different mindset. It sets the expectation that today we are plucked out of our everyday lives for something special.

I say I&#8217;m a Colorado wedding photographer, but I&#8217;m a transplant from the northeast and brought some of that mindset with me. I suppose the wedding photographer part of Colorado wedding photographer isn&#8217;t entirely accurate either. My background is in photojournalism and I continue to do documentary work. Those categories usually have little in common, but one should be defined more by the way they work than by the the particular story or client. I don&#8217;t change, much. Julia and David had their ceremony and reception at The Villa Parker . I&#8217;d worked as a Denver wedding photojournalist at The Villa Parker before and was familiar with the place.
