Colorado Springs Wedding Photography
 Colorado Springs is one of the most breathtaking places in the country to say your vows — with Pikes Peak as your backdrop, red rock formations framing your first look, and mile-high skies that shift from golden afternoon light to vivid alpenglow in a matter of minutes. At Candid Studios, we have been documenting weddings across Colorado since 2016, and we know that this city demands a photographer who is just as comfortable navigating a rustic mountain barn as they are working inside an elegant downtown ballroom. With more than 3,000 events captured, our team brings the experience, the gear, and the genuine passion for storytelling that your Colorado Springs wedding deserves.
 Wedding Photography Built Around Your Colorado Springs Day
 Every wedding package we offer starts with a pre-wedding consultation where we get to know you as a couple — your priorities, your timeline, and the specific moments you never want to forget. From there, we build a detailed shot list that works with your schedule rather than against it, so we are never pulling you away from your guests at the wrong moment. For larger ceremonies of 150 guests or more, we provide dual-photographer coverage to ensure nothing slips through the cracks, whether that is your partner&#x27;s expression during the vows or your grandmother wiping away a quiet tear in the third row.
We shoot with mirrorless full-frame cameras and fast prime lenses that are built for the low-light conditions common in Colorado Springs reception venues, where string lights and candlelight set the mood but can challenge lesser equipment. When the dance floor opens and the energy in the room peaks, you will not have to worry about blurry or dark frames. Our documentary approach means we direct when structure is needed — family formals, bridal portraits, the wedding party lineup — and then step back completely so genuine emotion can lead the way.
Every couple receives a professionally edited gallery of 400 to 800 images delivered within four weeks of your wedding day. Your private online gallery includes unlimited downloads and a full print release, so sharing with out-of-town family or ordering a heirloom album is completely straightforward. From your first consultation to the moment you open that gallery link, our team is here to support you every step of the way.
Colorado Springs Venues and Locations We Love to Photograph
 Colorado Springs offers an extraordinary variety of wedding settings, and our team has worked across the full range of what this region has to offer. The Garden of the Gods Club and Resort sits at the foot of those iconic sandstone formations, giving couples a backdrop that is honestly difficult to believe is real — the warm red rock against a blue Colorado sky photographs in a way that never gets old. Cheyenne Mountain Resort offers sweeping views of the Front Range and lush grounds that work beautifully for both intimate portraits and large bridal party shots during golden hour.
For couples drawn to historic architecture, the Broadmoor remains one of the most iconic wedding venues in all of Colorado, with its grand lakeside facade, ornate ballrooms, and manicured gardens that provide endless variety throughout a full coverage day. The Pinery at the Hill in downtown Colorado Springs blends exposed brick and warm industrial character with mountain-city charm, and the rooftop access creates portrait opportunities that are uniquely tied to this city. Further out, venues near Black Forest and Falcon offer rustic barn settings surrounded by towering ponderosa pines, giving your wedding day a true Colorado wilderness feel without sacrificing accessibility.
Beyond formal venues, Colorado Springs itself is a photographer&#x27;s landscape. Couples who want to incorporate engagement-style portraits into their wedding day often use stretches of the Pikes Peak Highway, the red rock trails near Manitou Springs, or the open meadows along the Palmer Park area. We are deeply familiar with the light, the terrain, and the seasonal conditions across all of these locations, which means we can guide you toward the right spots and the right timing to make your images exceptional.
Seasons, Weather, and What to Expect When You Marry in Colorado
 One of the most important things to understand about getting married in Colorado Springs is that the weather is genuinely unpredictable, and experienced photographers here know how to work with that rather than against it. Summer afternoons frequently bring fast-moving afternoon thunderstorms that roll in over the mountains, often clearing just in time for a dramatic sunset with moody skies and fresh light. We always build your shot list with weather flexibility in mind, identifying indoor backup locations at your venue and knowing exactly how to adjust the timeline if the sky has other plans.
Spring weddings in Colorado Springs often come with crisp air and wildflower color beginning to emerge along the foothills, while autumn is arguably the most spectacular season — the aspens turn gold across the higher elevations, the light drops lower and warmer, and the crowds at outdoor locations thin out considerably. Winter weddings near Pikes Peak carry their own quiet magic, particularly after a snowfall, when the landscape turns monochromatic and the contrast between a white gown and a snow-covered Garden of the Gods is simply stunning. At elevation, the light at midday can be harsh, so we time outdoor portrait sessions carefully to take full advantage of the softer, more flattering light in the hour or two before sunset.
Our team comes prepared for Colorado&#x27;s full range of conditions — UV filters for the intense high-altitude sun, fast glass for overcast or golden-hour shooting, and backup bodies so a gear issue never becomes your problem. We have photographed Colorado Springs weddings in every season and every kind of weather this state can produce, and that depth of local experience is something we bring to every couple who books with us.

Colorado Springs Wedding Photographer FAQ
How many hours of wedding photography coverage do you recommend for a Colorado Springs wedding? 
For most Colorado Springs weddings, we recommend a minimum of eight hours of coverage, which typically allows us to capture everything from bridal preparation through the first portion of your reception. If your ceremony is at a venue like the Broadmoor or Cheyenne Mountain Resort where getting-ready spaces and the ceremony site are spread out, or if you want dedicated time for portraits at a nearby location like the Garden of the Gods, ten to twelve hours gives us the room to do everything comfortably without rushing. During your pre-wedding consultation, we will map out your specific timeline and recommend the coverage window that fits your day.
What is included in your wedding photography packages? 
Every Candid Studios wedding photography package includes a pre-wedding consultation, a customized shot list built around your timeline, professional coverage on your wedding day, expert post-production editing, and a private online gallery with 400 to 800 fully edited images delivered within four weeks. Your gallery comes with unlimited downloads and a print release, so you can share images freely and order prints or albums from any provider you choose. For ceremonies with more than 150 guests, dual-photographer coverage is included to ensure complete, comprehensive documentation of your day.
How many edited photos will we receive from our Colorado Springs wedding? 
You can expect to receive between 400 and 800 professionally edited images, depending on the length of your coverage and the number of events within your wedding day. A full-day wedding at a venue like the Pinery at the Hill or a multi-location celebration that includes portrait time at a spot like Red Rock Canyon Open Space will naturally yield a larger gallery than a shorter, more intimate ceremony. Every image in your gallery is carefully reviewed and color-corrected — we do not simply bulk-deliver everything from the day, we curate a collection that genuinely tells your story from beginning to end.
Do you offer a second photographer for Colorado Springs weddings? 
Yes. For ceremonies with a guest count of 150 or more, dual-photographer coverage is a standard part of our packages. A second photographer allows us to capture simultaneous moments that a single shooter simply cannot cover — your expression and your partner&#x27;s expression at the same time during the first look, the wedding party and the guests reacting to your entrance, candid moments happening in different corners of the reception. For smaller weddings, a second photographer is available as an add-on, which is particularly worth considering if your venue has multiple levels or separate spaces, which is common at some of the larger Colorado Springs properties.
Is outdoor photography in Colorado Springs affected by altitude or weather, and how do you handle it? 
Altitude and weather are both very real considerations here, and our team is fully prepared for both. At Colorado Springs&#x27; elevation, the sun is significantly more intense than at sea level, which can affect both skin tones and how harsh midday shadows fall. We plan outdoor portrait sessions around the softer light in the late afternoon and early evening whenever possible. Summer afternoon thunderstorms are common along the Front Range, so we always identify covered or indoor backup options at your venue and build flexibility into your timeline. We also come equipped with gear suited to Colorado&#x27;s variable conditions, from bright mountain sunshine to overcast skies after a storm, so whatever the day brings, we are ready for it.
