Colorado&#8217;s Best Elopement Locations
From the dramatic red sandstone of Red Rocks to the snow-capped peaks of the Continental Divide, Colorado offers elopement settings that rival anywhere in the world. Boulder&#8217;s Flatirons provide a stunning backdrop just minutes from town, while Sapphire Point near Breckenridge delivers panoramic views of the Tenmile Range that will leave your guests speechless.
For couples craving a true mountain adventure, we love hiking to alpine lakes near Aspen or finding secluded meadows along the trails in Vail. Denver Botanic Gardens offers a lush, intimate alternative for couples who want natural beauty without the elevation gain. And as a self-solemnizing state, Colorado makes it beautifully simple to marry on your own terms.
Our team at From the Hip Photo knows these locations inside and out. We will help you find the perfect spot, navigate permits, and build a timeline that gives you the best light and the most privacy for your ceremony.
Your Elopement Experience
An elopement with From the Hip Photo is more than just a photo session. It is a thoughtfully planned experience built around your vision as a couple. We start by getting to know you, your story, your style, and the moments that matter most. Then we craft a plan that feels completely yours.
On your day, we are there to document everything with our signature documentary style: the nervous excitement before the ceremony, the raw emotion of your vows, the quiet moments between just the two of you, and the joyful celebration that follows. We work with natural light and real moments, creating images that feel genuine and alive.
Whether you want a sunrise ceremony on a remote peak or an afternoon exchange of vows at a scenic overlook, we bring the same energy, expertise, and passion to every elopement. Book your elopement consultation and let us help you plan something unforgettable.
Elopement Photography FAQ
What are the best locations for elopements in Colorado?
Colorado offers incredible elopement locations including Rocky Mountain National Park, the Flatirons near Boulder, Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre, Sapphire Point in Breckenridge, Maroon Bells near Aspen, and the stunning trails around Vail. Denver Botanic Gardens is also a popular choice for couples who want a beautiful setting close to the city. Each location provides a unique backdrop, from dramatic mountain peaks to lush alpine meadows. Our team at From the Hip Photo can help you find the perfect setting for your day.
How much does elopement photography cost in Colorado?
Elopement photography packages at From the Hip Photo are tailored to each couple&#8217;s needs and typically include travel time to locations within one hour of our Denver studio. Pricing varies based on coverage hours, location, and whether you need additional services like a second photographer but a good estimate is $800/hr for a one photographer session. Reach out for a custom quote that fits your vision and timeline.
Do you travel to mountain elopement locations outside of Denver?
Absolutely. We regularly travel to elopement locations throughout Colorado including Boulder, Aspen, Vail, Breckenridge, Estes Park, and beyond. Travel time is included for locations within one hour of our Denver studio, and we offer competitive travel rates for destinations further afield. We have extensive experience shooting in Colorado&#8217;s mountain terrain and know the best spots for every season.
What is the best time of year for a Colorado elopement?
Every season in Colorado offers something special for elopements. Summer and early fall bring wildflowers, golden aspens, and warm temperatures ideal for mountain hikes. Winter provides dramatic snowy landscapes perfect for intimate ceremonies. Spring offers fresh greenery and fewer crowds. The best time depends on your vision, whether you want alpine wildflowers, fall foliage, or a snowy mountain backdrop. Contact us to talk through the best season for your elopement.
Do we need a permit to elope in Colorado?
Many public lands and parks in Colorado require permits for elopement ceremonies, especially in popular locations like Rocky Mountain National Park and Red Rocks. Some locations have specific rules about group size, setup, and timing. We can help guide you through the permit process (even handling the permit application process on your behalf) and suggest locations that match your needs and budget. Colorado is also a self-solemnizing state, meaning you can legally marry yourselves without an officiant.
How long does an elopement photo session typically last?
Most elopement sessions last between two and four hours, giving us plenty of time to capture getting-ready moments, the ceremony, couple portraits at multiple locations, and any celebration afterward. For adventure elopements involving hikes to remote locations, we recommend longer sessions to allow for travel time and multiple scenic stops along the way. Book a consultation and we will help you build the perfect timeline.
