Frequently Asked Questions
Do you photograph LGBTQ+ weddings? 
Yes! I would hope it&#8217;s pretty obvious from my whole website that I love working with LGBTQ+ couples, but I also have a whole page on LGBTQ+ elopements here . 
Can you help us find a venue? 
I will recommend venues for your micro wedding, but it can sometimes be hard to find venues that fit your budget, fit your vision, and are available on the date you booked me for. Even for 20-30 person micro-weddings on weekdays, many venues in Colorado charge $5-15k+ for the day, so be open about your budget early in the planning process and I&#8217;ll do my best to find options that fit!
Can we have our micro-wedding at an airbnb? 
Getting married at a vacation rental is cooler in theory (but not if you force it to be). There are a handful of epic homes on airbnb and vrbo in Colorado that are suited for and allows a micro-weddings, but these come at a premium price and with limited availability. 
Most homes do not allow gatherings that include more people than the amount of guests on the lodging reservation. You can read more about airbnb weddings here .
How will you help us with planning? 
I help my couples with location guides, vendor recommendations, navigating permit requirements, and creating a timeline for the day. My couples also get a big planning guide with tips on wedding attire, adventure gear, activity ideas, lodging and travel, and everything else I’ve learned from planning and photographing over 280 weddings & elopements since 2017. 
I am not a wedding planner though. Here are just a few of the many ways in which a wedding planner can help you: 
Contact, book, and coordinate vendors and activities on your behalf
Help you design the day and make decisions on decor
Secure rentals (tables, chairs, dinnerware, barware, ceremony decor), pick everything up, and setup the ceremony and/or reception on the day of your elopement. 
Coordinate vendors on the day of your elopement, so that you don’t have to worry about missing calls from your chef or officiant.
If you’re interested in working with a planner, please ask me for recommendations! 
Is there a limit on how many guests we can have? 
Short answer: no—however many people you want to be a part of your wedding, there are ways to make it happen. But when guest counts get up over 30-40 people, you&#8217;re likely looking at a full wedding venue rental, and should budget accordingly. 
How far in advance should we book you? 
I generally don&#8217;t book more than 18 months in advance, and most of my couples book around 9-12 months out—but I&#8217;ve also booked an elopement the day before , so reach out no matter when you&#8217;re getting married. If I&#8217;m available, I&#8217;d love to help you plan and capture the day!
You do not need to have a specific date picked out before contacting me. If you want warm weather and wildflowers, fall colors, or a winter wonderland, I can help you pick the best date based on the location, weather, and avoiding any local events or holidays that can make a place more crowded than normal.
How do we book you? 
Get in touch via my contact form , and tell me all about what you want from your elopement, and what you&#8217;ve planned so far. If I’m available, we’ll schedule a video chat to talk more about your plans and how I can help you. This part is super important! The more comfortable you are around your photographer, the better your photos will be, too, so let&#8217;s make sure we&#8217;re a good fit before you book.
If you decide to work with me, I&#8217;ll send you a booking link with your contract, the invoice for the $2000 retainer, and once you&#8217;ve signed and paid it&#8217;s official and we&#8217;ll get started on planning your elopement. 
Do you shoot film? 
I primarily photograph my elopements on digital cameras, but I also use an Instax camera and a 35mm film camera for some highlight photos throughout the day. You can read more about my approach to film photography here .
When do we get our photos? 
You&#8217;ll get 5-10 sneak peek photos within a week after your wedding, and your full gallery will be ready within six weeks. 
Your photos are delivered in a digital gallery that you can easily share with family and friends. You can download your photos as many times as you want, as high resolution files without any watermarks. Your online gallery will be live for one year, so you&#8217;ll need to download and backup your photos elsewhere.
How many photos will we get? 
The exact number of photos will depend on how many hours I&#8217;m there for, and what&#8217;s happening throughout the day, but around 50 photos per hour of coverage is normal. You&#8217;ll get all the good stuff, without any unflattering photos or lots of duplicates of the exact same thing. 
Do you edit all the photos? 
Yes, every photo is edited in a style consistent with what you see on my website. I don&#8217;t retouch body shapes, hair, skin, or clothing, because I want you to look like yourselves in your photos!
How do you use AI in your business? 
I will never use AI to cull or edit your photos, and every word on this website and my social media was written by myself. Many photographers tout AI as a way to speed up their workflow, but I take a lot of pride in doing the work myself. 
Do you offer prints and albums? 
You can order high quality prints, wall art, cards, and wedding albums directly from your online gallery. All products ordered from your gallery come from my preferred printing labs, and are shipped directly to your home.
You&#8217;ll also get a print release that allows you to print your photos anywhere you&#8217;d like, but I can&#8217;t guarantee the colors and quality on any prints and products ordered outside of your gallery shop.
