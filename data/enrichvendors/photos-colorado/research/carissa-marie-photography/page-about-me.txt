A little introduction
I grew up in Texas, but when I was eight years old, my family spent our first summer in the San Juan Mountains. Those mountains became my second home – every summer we'd return to explore hidden alpine lakes, discover secret waterfalls, and hike every trail. Those summers shaped me into who I am today.
Years later, I fell into the world of wedding photography. I witnessed the stress and regrets of traditional weddings firsthand, with countless couples confiding in me that they wished they had done something different.
Then I got an inquiry that changed everything. 
A couple wanted to elope in the mountains with just them and their parents. They told me they wanted to elope somewhere that felt like a scene from The Sound of Music – and I immediately knew the perfect place.
Two months later I witnessed them exchange vows in complete solitude, surrounded by the wild landscape of the San Juan Mountains, and something clicked for me. 
That's when I knew exactly what I was meant to do with my life.
Now I get to help my couples elope in the same places that changed my life as a child
I get to help them experience that same feeling of being completely present in a place that makes you feel both small and infinite at the same time. 
To me, these mountains are so much more than just a pretty backdrop for photos. There's something deeply meaningful about helping couples begin their lives together in the landscapes that helped shape my understanding of beauty, love, and the power of wild places.
My connection to these mountains, combined with 7 years of experience documenting elopements, allows me to offer something truly unique: a deep level of love and reverence for the landscape that's evident in every single photograph.
You deserve a photographer who loves the place you choose to elope as much as you will after your wedding day.

 I eloped Too
I married Russell in a hidden spot on one of my favorite bumpy dirt roads in the San Juan Mountains.
We said private vows in an aspen grove as the leaves fell around us, before holding a ceremony with our closest people. My dad cried in front of me for one of the first times in my life as he pronounced us husband and wife. We drank hot cocoa with rum to warm up, and then held a dinner on the back porch of my parent's house, with string lights hanging from the trees around us. Russell and I wrote a hand written letter to each one of our guests, and then we enjoyed a dinner of gumbo cooked by my mom. My brother baked our cakes, and we popped a special bottle of champagne that Russell had been gifted by his dad years ago, and was holding on to for his wedding day.
Our day was filled with intention, laughter, tears, joy, and so much love. It was the best day of my life, and when you elope, it will be the best day of yours too, and I can't wait to help you bring it all to life.

I really love what I do
Yes, I’m a good photographer, and I provide a 5-star service, but I hope your biggest takeaway from all of this is that I am so unbelievably passionate about what I do.
I get told all the time that I have the coolest job in the world, and it’s true. And although it does come with some pretty awesome perks, like taking helicopter tours through the Alaskan wilderness, and eating wedding cake in breathtaking places, that’s not why I do this. I pursued this career because I knew it was what I was meant to do. 
I have found so much purpose in helping couples have a wedding day that honors the significance of marriage, because I know when you plan your day with thought and intention, and invest in the things that matter most, you’ll look back on it with fondness rather than the regrets that so many couples feel far too often. 
You’ll know that your money and time went towards the things that actually matter to you.
