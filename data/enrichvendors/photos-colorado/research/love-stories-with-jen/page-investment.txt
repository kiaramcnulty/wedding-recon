“If your pictures aren’t good enough, you’re not close enough.” — Robert Capa 
 This quote has always stuck with me; not just because it’s great photography advice, but because it perfectly captures how I see my work.
On the surface, it speaks to the physical act of getting closer to capture the details that tell the full story; the look in someone’s eye, the touch of a hand, the quiet in between moments that matter most. But most of the time, it’s about something deeper, getting close to the people I photograph.
I don’t believe in stiff poses or staying on the sidelines. I believe the best photos happen when people feel truly seen, safe, and themselves. That kind of closeness, comfort, and authenticity only comes when you’re willing to get close in a more personal way. That means building trust, creating space for you to feel fully like yourselves, and always letting your real connection lead the way. I want you to feel like I’m a friend with a camera, not a stranger observing from afar. It is this closeness that allows me to capture love stories as honestly and beautifully as they deserve to be remembered.
The result are photos that feel like you. Real, raw, quiet, loud, messy, magical; whatever your love looks like, that’s what we’ll capture.

why 35mm/120 film?
In the digital age where 100s of photos can be taken in the split of a second, film makes me notice and slow down. It’s all about being intentional with every shot since they are numbered. Film is a chemical miracle, a physical representation of how the light of the moment interacted with the film itself. It cannot be altered, and in this time where everything is perfectly manicured and manufactured with ai, this level of authenticity is just beautiful. Film is inherently nostalgic and will only get better over time. To me, film photos show not only how the moment looked, but more importantly how it felt. Film will always be included in my packages and is a necessary part of my creative process, most of the photos you see on this website are film!
