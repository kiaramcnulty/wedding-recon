A Wedding In The Clouds: San Sophia Overlook
There are places that leave you breathless, where the scenery seems to mirror the magic of the moment. San Sophia Overlook in Telluride, Colorado , is one of those rare wedding venues. As a wedding photographer who has photographed over 100 weddings all over the country, I can honestly say that San Sophia is nothing short of spectacular.
Arriving in the Sky
The journey to San Sophia begins with a gondola ride unlike any other. Your guests step aboard in the heart of Telluride or Mountain Village and glide silently above pine forests and alpine meadows. The ride itself feels like an adventure, a slow and beautiful ascent to a ceremony site suspended between earth and sky. Side note, my husband actually proposed to me on this very gondola in Telluride over 12 years ago. 🙂
When the gondola doors open at 10,540 feet, the world feels transformed. Crisp mountain air greets you, and in every direction, peaks rise like jagged cathedral spires. It’s a setting that immediately hushes you with its grandeur. 
The walk up to the San Sophia overlook deck takes approximately 5-7 minutes depending on if you&#8217;re used to the altitude or not! There are vans that can also be arranged to drive some of your guests that may need the extra help! Trust me, it&#8217;s worth the journey once you get up to the overlook. 
The Ceremony Setting
The ceremony deck at San Sophia Overlook is intentionally simple. This understated design allows the San Juan Mountains to take center stage and for you to be able to create the wedding of your dreams with all of the florals and decor you can imagine to add to the natural beauty. San Sophia weddings appear as if they could be in a movie. 
Seasons of Beauty
Each season brings a new personality to San Sophia Overlook, offering couples the chance to match their love story to the rhythm of the mountains.
Summer : Daisies scatter across the hillside and in June, Wilson Peak is often still covered in snow. The sky seems endless, and sunsets bathe the peaks in warm golden light.
Fall : The aspen groves that you will be gazing upon are brilliant shades of gold. It’s a painter’s dream come to life. Fall in Telluride is simply awe inspiring and easily my favorite time of year to photograph weddings.
Winter : A snow-draped wonderland transforms the overlook into something straight from a fairytale. Ceremonies here feel intimate and magical, as if sealed inside a snow globe. One caveat is that you do have to wait until the ski mountain closes to begin your ceremony. Telluride ski resort closes at 4pm, so typically ceremony start time is at 5pm. I mention this because it is SIGNIFICANTLY better to do a winter wedding in late February through the 1st week of April as the sunset is later, giving you more daylight on the mountain.
No matter the season, San Sophia’s beauty is raw, untamed, and unforgettable.
The Celebration Continues
Because San Sophia Overlook is a ceremony-only venue , receptions are typically held nearby. Fortunately, Telluride and Mountain Village offer stunning options just a gondola ride away.
Couples often choose Allred’s Restaurant , perched at the gondola station with floor-to-ceiling windows and views of the valley below—perfect for an elegant, candlelit dinner. Others opt for Gorrono Ranch , Tomboy Tavern, Timber room and The Peaks Resort & Spa .
