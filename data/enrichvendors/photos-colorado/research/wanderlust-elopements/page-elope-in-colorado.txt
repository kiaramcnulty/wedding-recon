Colorado Mountain Elopement Locations
Colorado is home to some of the most breathtaking trails, making it the perfect destination for an adventure elopement. Can you picture exchanging vows on a secluded ridge along the Colorado Trail, or beneath the aspens of the Maroon Bells? If you are seeking dramatic alpine views, Ice Lakes, Blue Lakes, and Sky Pond offer crystal-clear waters and rugged mountain backdrops. Whether you want to hike through wildflower meadows in Crested Butte or catch a sunset over the San Juan Mountains, eloping in Colorado will create an unforgettable setting for an adventure elopement.

Your Colorado Elopement Photographers - FAQs 
Do we need a marriage license to elope in Colorado? 
Yes, you’ll need to apply for a marriage license at any Colorado County Clerk's office. However, Colorado allows self-solemnizing, so you don’t need an officiant or witnesses to make it legal. Yay! 
What’s the best time of year to elope in Colorado? 
Spring/early Summer are great for lower elevations; lots of water running aka waterfalls! Summer (July–September) is ideal for high-elevation locations due to snowmelt, while fall (late September–early October) offers stunning golden aspens. Winter elopements can be magical but require extra planning for snow and cold.
Do we need a permit to elope in certain locations? 
Some national and state parks, including Rocky Mountain National Park and Maroon Bells, require permits for ceremonies and photography. Having done this many times before, we know the ins and outs of this! 
How much hiking is involved for an adventure elopement? 
That is totally up to you! We’re avid hikers, so we are willing to go anywhere you desire. Some spots require only a short walk, while others involve strenuous hikes. We will find the perfect location for you based on your comfort level. 
What’s the Leave No Trace policy for elopements? 
We ALWAYS follow Leave No Trace principles by staying on trails, packing out our trash, and respecting wildlife and plant life. We adore and respect the Colorado landscape and do our best to keep it pristine.
