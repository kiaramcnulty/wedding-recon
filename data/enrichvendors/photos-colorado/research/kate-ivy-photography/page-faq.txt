F A Q 
Question 
Do you shoot film?
Answer 
Do you travel?
100% yes!! I am based out of Denver but I have traveled to over 30 countries and I am always up for an adventure. If you are thinking of eloping and want some ideas please let me know! I have a bucket list and spots that I am familiar with. I try to keep things as simple as possible as far as travel fees go. Let’s chat! 
How many photos do I get?
I deliver 50-100 high resolution edited photos for every hour I am shooting. So if you hire me for 8 hours of coverage you will receive 400-800 photos. It is a large spectrum but with every wedding being different - if there are only 25 guest you probably don’t want or need 70 photos of Aunt Cindy dancing :) I usually end up delivering around 600 - 700 photos for an 8 hour wedding. 
Yes! Film can be added to any package and is included in the elopement package.
When do I get my photos?
Photos are delivered via a website with unlimited downloads 7-8 weeks after the wedding. I also send a pretty big sneak peak of some favorites the week after the wedding. This will hold you over and give you some options if you want to send out “thank yous” or post on Instagram.
Is there a deposit? 
Upon booking and signing of the contract, 25% of the 50% non-refundable retainer is due to confirm the wedding date. And the other 25% is due 90 days later. The retainer goes toward the entire wedding balance. The remaining balance is due 21 days before the wedding. In the event of the clients cancelling the wedding for any reason the booking fee is non-refundable. If the wedding date is moved within 6 months and I am available the retainer will be applied to the new date. Lots of COVID clauses! Let’s work together. 
Do you help with the timeline?
Yes please!! The first thing I do when considering a timeline and how many hours you might need is look up the exact time the sun is setting that day. If it’s November it could be 4:30pm if its June it could be 8:30pm - this will help us decided when to do the couples portion of the day, taking into consideration a first look and family photos. Family photos usually take around 25-30 minutes. Wedding party about 20 minutes. And I recommend allowing a total of about one hour for couples’ photos. This could be a 30 min first look and then 30 minutes at “golden hour.” For some couples, they really want to join the cocktail hour - so this is something to consider as well! This is your day - but I do believe part of my job is to make sure you get the photos YOU want! And with the best lighting. 
Do you deliver RAW unedited photos? 
No. A big part of why you are hiring me is because of my editing style. I consider my photos artwork - and I wouldn’t put out an unfinished piece of artwork. I carefully cull through every photo taken and make sure I don’t leave out anything important! Most of the photos that don’t make it are either unflattering, not in focus, or a double of another photo. 
Do you help with location scouting? 
Absolutely! Many couples I photograph are not from Colorado - they have ideas of what they want, but haven’t scouted for that perfect spot to elope or do an engagement session. We can talk about your vision and I can throw out a ton of ideas with galleries to share. Or we can pick a new spot none of us have been to. I consider the weather and the time of year. Permits and how crowded somewhere might be. Time of day and the day of the week. 
Do you do Video? 
Unfortunately, I only offer photography! But I work with some amazing videographers - you can hire them through me for a seamless package that includes both or I can share my vendor list and you can hire them separately. 
Based in Denver, Kate Ivy Photography is led by a pair of Colorado wedding photographers who specialize in elopements, micro weddings, engagements, adventure sessions, and lifestyle photography across the Colorado Rockies and destinations worldwide.
