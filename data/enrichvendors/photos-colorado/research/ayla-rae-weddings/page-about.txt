About Ayla 
pronounced “eye-luh”
My first foray into wedding photography was as a photo assistant for a company that photographed extravagant weddings with hundreds of guests. The couples were exhausted by the time the ceremony started.
I thought, "This can't possibly be what a wedding is supposed to feel like," then promptly did a dramatic 180 and only photographed elopements for a few years.
Connection over Everything
In working these giant luxury events and then only photographing the tiniest, simplest weddings, what I was really looking to create in my work was connection.
Connection with you, your families, your histories, so that I can tell your story properly on one of the most emotional days of your lives.
speaking of Connection, here's a bit about me.
I have a giant extended family and a blended immediate family (one brother, two step siblings, and three siblings-in-law). Dinners are always loud and of course, not a single good, recent, iPhone accessible photo of all of us together exists. Closest I could find is this one of me and my sisters.
I was raised in both Colorado and Oregon by road-trip loving parents. Thanks to those road trips, I've visited over half the United States. I love to travel, and odds are, I probably have family where you're from.
My husband and I eloped on the coast, then had our wedding at a brewery on the Columbia River outside of Portland, Oregon. 
 We, too, wanted a wedding that didn't feel like a wedding, and we ended up having the most wonderful beer, tacos, and dance party (all of our favorite things) on a mountain-ringed patio.

My couples have married at classic city venues, mountain chateaus, breweries, restaurants, gardens, on family land. They've had heaps of flowers, zero flowers, private ceremonies, pickle-back shots after their vows, surprise Malort shots at the reception, guest performances, a mid-day break at their local coffeeshop. 
 No matter which traditions you're sticking with or which trends you're dodging, I'm here to support you with seasoned experience, calm presence, and an artist’s way of seeing things.
