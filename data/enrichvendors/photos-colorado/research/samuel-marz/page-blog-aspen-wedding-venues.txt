Aspen Wedding Venues: 7 Beautiful Places to Get Married in Aspen Colorado
 Aspen is one of the most iconic places in Colorado to get married. Surrounded by dramatic alpine peaks, historic hotels, and private ranches tucked into the mountains, it offers couples an incredible mix of luxury and natural beauty.
 Whether you’re planning a destination wedding weekend or an intimate celebration in the mountains, Aspen has some of the most beautiful venues in the country.
 This guide highlights seven of the best Aspen wedding venues along with venue details, typical costs, and insights to help you choose the perfect location for your Colorado mountain wedding.

The Little Nell 
 Website: https://www.thelittlenell.com 
 Address: 675 E Durant Ave, Aspen, CO 81611
 Phone: (970) 920-4600
 The Little Nell is one of the most luxurious wedding venues in Aspen and one of the few ski-in, ski-out five-star hotels in the world. Many couples choose The Little Nell for its combination of mountain scenery, elevated dining, and exceptional service.
 Ceremonies often take place on Aspen Mountain, where guests ride the gondola to reach sweeping alpine views above town.
 Style of venue: Luxury mountain resort
 Typical venue cost: $20,000–$60,000 depending on event size
 Review highlight:
 “The Little Nell created the most incredible wedding experience for us and our guests. The mountain views and service were truly unforgettable.”
 T-Lazy-7 Ranch 
 Website: https://www.tlazy7.com 
 Address: 3129 Maroon Creek Rd, Aspen, CO 81611
 Phone: (970) 925-4614
 T-Lazy-7 Ranch is one of the most beloved ranch wedding venues near Aspen. Located just outside town on the road to Maroon Bells, it offers wide open meadows, mountain views, and a rustic barn reception space.
 Couples who want a relaxed Colorado ranch celebration often fall in love with the setting here.
 Style of venue: Rustic mountain ranch
 Typical venue cost: $10,000–$25,000 venue rental
 Review highlight:
 “The ranch was absolutely beautiful and felt so authentic to Colorado. Our ceremony view of the mountains was incredible.”
 Aspen Meadows Resort 
 Website: https://www.aspenmeadows.com 
 Address: 845 Meadows Rd, Aspen, CO 81611
 Phone: (970) 544-7824
 Aspen Meadows Resort sits on forty acres of open space just outside downtown Aspen. The venue is known for its modern Bauhaus architecture and sweeping views of the surrounding mountains.
 With multiple indoor and outdoor event spaces, it offers flexibility for both intimate weddings and larger celebrations.
 Style of venue: Modern mountain resort
 Typical venue cost: $15,000–$35,000 depending on guest count
 Review highlight:
 “Aspen Meadows felt peaceful and scenic while still being close to town. Our ceremony backdrop was breathtaking.”
 Hotel Jerome 
 Website: https://aubergeresorts.com/hoteljerome 
 Address: 330 E Main St, Aspen, CO 81611
 Phone: (970) 920-1000
 Hotel Jerome is one of Aspen’s most historic and iconic hotels. Built in the late 1800s, it blends western charm with modern luxury.
 Couples often choose Hotel Jerome for its intimate atmosphere and elegant reception spaces in the heart of Aspen.
 Style of venue: Historic luxury hotel
 Typical venue cost: $15,000–$40,000 depending on event size
 Review highlight:
 “Hotel Jerome gave our wedding such a warm and elegant feeling. It was the perfect mix of history and luxury.”
 Pine Creek Cookhouse 
 Website: https://pinecreekcookhouse.com 
 Address: 11399 Castle Creek Rd, Aspen, CO 81611
 Phone: (970) 925-1044
 Pine Creek Cookhouse offers one of the most scenic wedding settings near Aspen. Located in the Castle Creek Valley, the venue is surrounded by dramatic peaks and open fields.
 It’s an ideal venue for couples who want a quiet mountain ceremony with incredible scenery.
 Style of venue: Scenic mountain restaurant venue
 Typical venue cost: $10,000–$20,000 depending on guest count
 Review highlight:
 “The setting at Pine Creek Cookhouse was absolutely breathtaking. It felt private and truly magical.”
 Aspen Mountain Club 
 Website: https://www.aspenmountainclub.com 
 Address: Aspen Mountain, Aspen, CO
 Aspen Mountain Club is a private club venue located on Aspen Mountain. Couples can host ceremonies with panoramic mountain views and receptions overlooking the valley below.
 The venue feels both exclusive and scenic, making it a unique location for destination weddings.
 Style of venue: Private mountain club
 Typical venue cost: varies depending on event scale
 Review highlight:
 “Our guests were blown away by the views from Aspen Mountain Club. It felt incredibly special.”
 Maroon Bells Amphitheater 
 Website: https://www.fs.usda.gov 
 Address: Maroon Creek Rd, Aspen, CO
 Maroon Bells Amphitheater is one of the most photographed ceremony locations in the United States. With the famous Maroon Bells peaks rising behind Maroon Lake, ceremonies here feel incredibly dramatic and unforgettable.
 Because it’s located within a protected wilderness area, weddings here are typically small and require permits.
 Style of venue: Outdoor alpine ceremony site
 Typical venue cost: $200–$500 permit fee
 Review highlight:
 “Getting married at Maroon Bells was a once in a lifetime experience. The scenery was absolutely breathtaking.”
 Average Cost of a Wedding in Aspen
 Aspen weddings are often among the most expensive in Colorado due to the destination nature of the area and luxury venues.
 Many couples hosting weddings in Aspen spend between $70,000 and $200,000+ depending on the venue, guest count, and level of production involved.
 Private ranch weddings and luxury resort celebrations can easily exceed this range when multi-day events are included.
 Frequently Asked Questions
 What is the best month to get married in Aspen?
 June through September are the most popular months due to warm weather and wildflowers. Fall weddings in late September can also be stunning when the aspens turn gold.
 How far in advance should we book an Aspen wedding venue?
 Most Aspen venues book 12–18 months in advance, especially for peak summer weekends.
 Are outdoor ceremonies common in Aspen?
 Yes. Many couples choose outdoor ceremony locations because of the mountain views, though backup plans are always recommended.
 Are Aspen weddings typically destination weddings?
 Yes. Most couples and guests travel to Aspen for weddings, which often turn into full wedding weekend experiences.
