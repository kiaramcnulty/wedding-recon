What to consider when you're deciding on a wedding dress from a wedding photographers experience! 
Choosing a wedding dress is a big decision, and there are several factors to consider to ensure you find the one that makes you feel beautiful, comfortable, and confident on your special day. Here are some key things to think about from a wedding photographers experience:
1. Your Personal Style 
 Taste & Personality : Think about the style of clothing you typically wear. Do you like classic, romantic, bohemian, or modern looks? The wedding dress should feel like an extension of who you are, so find a style that resonates with your personality. Something to note, a wedding dress with a bit of flare can add a fun, dynamic element to your photos, whether it be a long train, funky sleeves or fun vail. 
Fabric Preference : Different fabrics evoke different vibes. Lace, silk, satin, chiffon, and tulle all have distinct textures and drape in unique ways. Consider what feels comfortable and aligns with your style. Note that tulle really attracts bugs if you are getting married outside :) And satin wrinkles pretty easily and due to its smooth texture and reflective quality, satin tends to show water droplets distinctly.
2. Wedding Theme and Venue 
 Location & Season : Your wedding venue and the season can play a significant role in choosing your dress. For example, if you're having a beach wedding, something lightweight and flowy might work best, while a winter wedding might call for something with more structure and warmth.
Formality : Consider the level of formality of the wedding. A more traditional, formal ceremony might call for a ball gown or A-line dress, while a more relaxed or informal celebration could be perfect for a simpler, more modern gown.
3. Body Shape and Fit 
 Silhouette : Wedding dresses come in various silhouettes like A-line, mermaid, ball gown, and sheath. Choose one that complements your body type and makes you feel confident. For example:
 A-line: Universally flattering and great for most body types.
Ball gown: Ideal for those wanting a princess look or a more dramatic statement.
Mermaid: Fitted and curves-enhancing, suited for hourglass figures.
Sheath: Elegant and streamlined, often best for a slimmer figure.
Comfort & Fit : Make sure the dress is not only flattering but also comfortable enough to wear for hours. You’ll be moving, sitting, and dancing, so think about how the dress fits and feels during those activities. Choose a wedding dress that enhances your best features and makes you feel comfortable, rather than drawing attention to areas you're less confident about.
4. Budget 
 Set a Budget : Wedding dresses can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars, so it’s important to establish a budget upfront and stick to it. Don't forget to include alterations, accessories, and the cost of a veil or other bridal accessories. Used wedding dress sites are a really great option as well! 
Consider Customization : If you’re looking for a custom or couture gown, that will add to the cost. However, it could be worth the investment if you're looking for something unique.
5. Alterations 
 Fitting and Adjustments : Most brides need some alterations, whether it's adjusting the hem, taking in the bodice, or adding custom elements like sleeves. Make sure you factor in time and costs for these changes when purchasing your dress.
Timeframe : Allow at least 6-8 months for the dress to be made and altered properly. Many bridal boutiques require several fittings to ensure the perfect fit.
6. Trends vs. Timelessness 
 Classic vs. Trendy : Some brides love to follow the latest trends, while others prefer a timeless, classic look. Keep in mind that trends come and go, so think about whether you’ll love the dress in 10 years. Select a dress that reflects your unique personality and makes you feel true to yourself. 
Timeless Touch : If you're unsure about trends, a timeless look—such as a lace dress with a sweetheart neckline or a simple silk gown—can always look elegant and stay relevant over time.
7. Your Bridal Party 
Coordinating with Bridesmaids : If you have a bridal party, consider how your dress coordinates with the bridesmaids’ dresses. You don’t have to match perfectly, but it’s nice to have a cohesive look in terms of color, formality, and overall aesthetic.
8. Feelings & Intuition 
Emotional Connection : Many brides describe the moment they find "the one" as an emotional experience. Trust your instincts. When you find the right dress, it should make you feel beautiful, excited, and confident.
9. Try Different Styles 
Don't Settle Too Soon : You may have an idea of what you want, but sometimes a dress you never considered might surprise you. It's good to try on different silhouettes and styles to see what flatters you best.
Incorporate Accessories : Sometimes accessories (a veil, jewelry, shoes, etc.) can completely transform the look of a wedding dress. Consider how they will enhance the overall vibe of your gown.
10. Practical Considerations 
 Weather : If you're getting married in the summer, a lightweight dress might be essential, while a heavier gown might be perfect for a colder climate or a winter wedding.
Mobility : Think about how easy it will be to walk, sit, and dance in your dress. Some gowns are very structured, which can limit movement, while others are designed for comfort and ease of movement. From a photographer’s perspective, dress mobility is crucial for capturing fluid, natural shots. A gown that allows for ease of movement ensures you can strike beautiful poses, walk gracefully, and dance freely, all while maintaining the elegance and flow of the dress in every frame. Structured dresses create sharp, defined lines that add a sense of elegance and sophistication in photographs. They hold their shape beautifully, allowing for dramatic, timeless shots that highlight the gown’s intricate details and the bride's posture, making for striking, high-impact images.
Final Thoughts:
 Choosing a wedding dress is a deeply personal experience, and the most important thing is that you feel incredible in the gown you choose. Remember that your wedding dress is just one part of your day—what matters most is how you feel in it and the love and memories that surround it. Take your time, enjoy the process, and trust that you'll find a dress that makes you feel like the best version of yourself on your big day!
