GET TO KNOW US
Elliot has been doing this since 2016 and the work is still surprising and fresh, which is probably because no two people do this the same way. The couples who book us tend to know exactly who they are; they're just looking for someone who can keep up. We can!
 Our approach is photojournalistic, which means the day belongs to you and we’re just there to record what actually happens in it. After a decade of this, we can tell you that the photographs people go back to are never the ones we staged — they're the split-second glance, the laugh that got away from someone, the quiet exhale right before everything changed. Those moments don't happen when people are performing. They happen when someone is finally just there. Our job is to create enough safety and enough stillness that you forget to be anywhere else.
On our signature style: we’re not interested in washed-out, everybody-looks-the-same presets. We shoot in color because color is honest — bold, specific, and true to the room. We can go moody when the day calls for it. What we won't do is flatten your photographs into something generic because generic was easier. You didn't plan a generic day.
THE EXPERIENCE 
What it’s like to work together 
( 01 ) 
Before we begin
Every shoot starts with a real conversation. What you want, what you've hated about being photographed before, what this needs to feel like when it's done. Ten years of doing this has taught me that the work before the camera is half the work.
( 02 ) 
The day itself
Calm, prepared, and impossible to rattle. Whether it's a mountain elopement at altitude, a small business shoot in a cramped office, or a wedding day where three things went wrong before noon — I've got it. I've been photographing professionally long enough that very little qualifies as a problem anymore.
( 03 ) 
After it’s done
Your gallery arrives fast, edited with intention, and ready to use — whether that's on your wall, your website, your Instagram, or all three. Every image finished. Every skin tone rendered correctly. No presets applied uniformly to people who deserved better.
