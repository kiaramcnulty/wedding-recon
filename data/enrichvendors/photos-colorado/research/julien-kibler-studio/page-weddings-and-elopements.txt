Elopement FAQ.
How long should we plan for our elopement coverage? 
While timing varies based on your vision, I typically recommend at least 4 hours to capture getting ready moments, your ceremony, couple’s portraits, and any post-ceremony celebrations. We’ll create a custom timeline that ensures a relaxed, unhurried experience.
What are the best months for Telluride elopement photography? 
As a Telluride elopement photographer, I recommend June through October for the most versatile conditions. Late June through August offers the warmest weather and access to high-alpine locations like Bridal Veil Falls, perfect for couples wanting dramatic mountain backdrops without snow concerns. September is my personal favorite for Telluride elopement photography – you’ll get stunning golden aspens, crisp mountain air, and fewer crowds, creating that intimate atmosphere most couples crave. Early October can be magical with fall colors, though weather becomes more unpredictable. Winter elopements (December-March) offer a completely different but equally breathtaking experience, with snow-covered peaks and cozy downtown Telluride vibes, though location options become more limited.
What if it rains or snows? 
Mountain weather is dynamic! I monitor conditions closely and always have backup locations ready. Plus, some of the most dramatic and beautiful photos come from embracing the weather – think moody clouds or snow-capped peaks!
Can we bring family or a few friends? 
Yes! While elopements are typically intimate, you’re welcome to include a small group of loved ones. I’ll help create a timeline that balances private moments with group celebrations.
How do we choose our elopement location? 
Unless you have a specific place in mind, after booking, we’ll have a consultation call to discuss your vision. I’ll then share my location guide featuring hidden gems across Western Colorado, from alpine lakes to aspen groves, helping you find the perfect spot that matches your style and accessibility needs.
Do we need permits for Telluride elopement locations? 
Great question! As your Telluride elopement photographer, I’ll guide you through the permit process. Most popular Telluride elopement spots require permits for ceremonies, even intimate ones. Uncompahgre National Forest locations (including many alpine areas around Telluride) require special use permits for weddings, which typically cost $75-150 and take 2-4 weeks to process. Town of Telluride parks like Telluride Town Park also require permits for ceremonies. However, some private venues and certain backcountry locations don’t require permits. The good news? I work with these permit processes regularly and can help you navigate the requirements, suggest permit-free alternatives, or connect you with locations that handle permitting for you. I always ensure we’re compliant so you can focus on your special day.
When should we book you for our elopement? 
Popular dates, especially during peak seasons (summer and fall), often book 6-12 months in advance. However, I occasionally have availability for last-minute elopements, so don’t hesitate to ask!
What makes mountain elopements unique? 
Mountain elopements offer unparalleled flexibility and intimacy. Whether you want a sunrise ceremony at 13,000 feet or a sunset celebration in a wildflower meadow, we can create an experience that’s authentically you – no need to follow traditional wedding rules!
How does altitude affect our Telluride mountain elopement? 
Telluride sits at 8,750 feet, and many of our most stunning elopement locations reach 10,000-13,000+ feet – this definitely impacts your day! As an experienced Telluride elopement photographer, I recommend arriving 2-3 days early to acclimate, especially if you’re coming from sea level. You might experience shortness of breath, fatigue, or mild headaches initially. Stay extra hydrated (the dry mountain air is deceptive) and avoid alcohol the night before your elopement. I always plan extra time in our timeline for rest breaks and slower movement at high altitudes. The good news? The altitude creates incredibly crisp, clear air that makes for stunning photography – those deep blue skies and sharp mountain details you see in Telluride elopement photos are partly thanks to our elevation! I’ll also suggest lower-altitude backup locations if needed, ensuring your day stays perfect regardless of how you’re feeling.
We're not from Colorado - can you help us choose the perfect season/date? 
Absolutely! Each season in the San Juans offers something magical – from wildflower-filled meadows in summer to golden aspens in fall, dramatic snow-capped peaks in winter, and vibrant new growth in spring. I’ll help you choose the perfect time based on your vision, desired locations, and activities.
