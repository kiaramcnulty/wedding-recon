Wedding and Event Photography and Videography in Fort Collins, Colorado
 The Fort Collins Wedding and Event Scene
 Fort Collins has a character all its own, and it shows up in the weddings and events celebrated here. This is a college town with a brewery culture and a genuine love of the outdoors, where the Cache la Poudre River spills out of the canyon, the foothills rise just west of town, and Old Town&#x27;s historic brick storefronts give the whole place a warmth that photographs beautifully. Couples come here for that exact mix of small-city charm and Front Range backdrop, and the celebrations reflect it: relaxed, personal, and rooted in a place people are proud to call home. Candid Studios has been documenting weddings and events across the country since 2016, and we bring that same documentary instinct to every Fort Collins celebration, whether it is an intimate gathering or a full reception that runs late into the night.
Fort Collins couples celebrate at venues like The Elizabeth Hotel in Old Town Fort Collins, Windsong Estate Event Center in east Fort Collins, and The Preserve at Bingham Hill near Bellvue. Each one offers a distinct feeling to work with. The Elizabeth Hotel sits right in the heart of Old Town, steps from the walkable streets, string lights, and historic architecture that make for effortless portrait backdrops between the ceremony and reception. Windsong Estate Event Center, set out to the east, gives couples room to spread out with open sky and a more expansive, garden-and-estate feel. The Preserve at Bingham Hill, tucked near Bellvue at the edge of the foothills, leans into the natural Colorado landscape, with the kind of wide-open western light and rolling terrain that pairs sweeping scenery with close, candid moments. These are the kinds of spaces where the Fort Collins wedding scene comes to life, and we are comfortable shooting weddings and events at indoor and outdoor venues of any size or style, so wherever in the Fort Collins area you are celebrating, a coordinated team can be there. During your consultation, we map a timeline and shot list around the specific venue and neighborhoods that define your day, from Old Town&#x27;s brick alleys to the foothills west of town.
Seasons, Climate, and Logistics for Shooting in Fort Collins
 Planning a Fort Collins celebration means planning around the weather honestly, and that is something we build into every timeline. Peak wedding season here runs May through September, with daytime temperatures in the 70s and 80s, and June and September are especially popular for their comfortable, stable conditions. Those shoulder months are a sweet spot: warm enough for outdoor ceremonies and portraits, but without the sharpest edge of midsummer heat. We love what late-day light does across the Front Range during this stretch, when the sun drops toward the foothills and the whole landscape goes warm and directional in the final hour before sunset.
The one thing every Fort Collins couple should plan for is summer weather. Afternoons carry a real risk of fast-moving thunderstorms and occasional hail along the Front Range, the kind that can build over the mountains and arrive quickly. That is not a reason to avoid an outdoor ceremony, but it is a reason to plan a backup. We talk through indoor or covered contingencies with every couple ahead of time and keep a flexible shooting plan so that a passing storm becomes part of the story rather than a disruption. Some of the most striking images come in the clear, dramatic minutes right after a Front Range storm rolls through, when the air is washed clean and the light turns extraordinary.
Logistically, Fort Collins is compact and walkable in its core, which makes a downtown timeline easy to manage, but venues like Windsong Estate and The Preserve at Bingham Hill sit outside the center, so we factor travel time into the day when a ceremony and reception are split between locations. High-altitude UV along the Front Range is strong even on mild days, and reception light shifts fast once the sun is down, so our cameras and lenses are chosen to perform across bright outdoor midday, golden-hour portraits, and low-light receptions alike. Whatever date you choose, we plan coverage around the local light and weather so the day flows and nothing important gets missed.
