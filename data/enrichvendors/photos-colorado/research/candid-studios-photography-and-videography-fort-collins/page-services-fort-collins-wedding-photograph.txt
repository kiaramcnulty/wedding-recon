Fort Collins Wedding Photography
 Fort Collins is one of Colorado&#x27;s most genuinely livable cities — a place where the foothills meet a thriving downtown, where craft culture runs deep, and where couples who choose to marry here tend to have strong opinions about doing things their own way. Candid Studios has spent years photographing weddings across Colorado, and we know that a Fort Collins wedding calls for a photographer who can move from a sun-drenched outdoor ceremony along the Cache la Poudre River to a candlelit reception inside a converted historic building without missing a single honest moment. With more than 3,000 events captured since 2016, we bring the experience and equipment to keep up with wherever your day takes us.
 Fort Collins Venues We Love to Photograph
 Fort Collins offers a range of wedding settings that give photographers genuinely interesting light and architecture to work with. The Lyric on the edge of Old Town is a favorite for couples who want an intimate, music-infused atmosphere with warm interior tones that reward fast prime lenses. Edora Park and the surrounding Poudre River Trail corridor give outdoor ceremonies a backdrop of cottonwoods and open Colorado sky, especially beautiful during the golden hours of a late-spring or early-autumn afternoon.
For couples drawn to the agricultural character of the region, the venues along Harmony Road and out toward Windsor blend working-ranch aesthetics with mountain views that make wide-angle portraits look effortless. The Armstrong Hotel in the heart of Old Town offers a boutique historic setting where the brick walls and vintage lighting create a mood that practically photographs itself during cocktail hour.
City Park and Library Park in the residential heart of Fort Collins are popular for elopements and small ceremonies, with the mature elm canopy providing soft, diffused light even on a bright Colorado afternoon. Whatever Fort Collins venue you choose, we scout it with your timeline in mind so we know exactly where the best light will fall during your first look and your exit.
Photographing Fort Collins Weddings Through Every Season
 Colorado&#x27;s Front Range keeps photographers on their toes, and Fort Collins is no exception. Summers here are warm and sun-saturated, which is wonderful for outdoor portraits along the Poudre River, but it also means midday ceremonies can produce harsh shadows that require careful positioning and reflectors. We plan first-look timing with the sun angle in mind so your portraits have the soft, directional light they deserve rather than the flat overhead glare of a noon ceremony.
Fall is arguably the most spectacular season for Fort Collins weddings. The cottonwoods and aspens turn gold and amber from late September through mid-October, and the afternoon light at that latitude takes on a warmth and depth that is difficult to replicate any other time of year. Horsetooth Reservoir and the foothills west of town become a natural portrait studio during this period.
Winter weddings in Fort Collins carry their own quiet drama. A fresh snow along Mountain Avenue or out near the foothills creates a clean, high-contrast backdrop, and the low winter sun stays in the golden-hour range for much of the afternoon. Spring brings unpredictable weather — we have photographed ceremonies in light snow flurries in April and in 70-degree sunshine the very next weekend — so our kit always includes weather-sealed gear and a contingency eye for beautiful indoor light whenever Colorado decides to surprise everyone.
What to Expect from a Candid Studios Wedding Package
 Every Fort Collins wedding we photograph begins with a pre-wedding consultation where we talk through your venue, your timeline, your family dynamics, and the specific moments that matter most to you. From that conversation we build a detailed shot list that accounts for Fort Collins-specific considerations — travel time between Old Town and an outdoor ceremony site, the way afternoon shadows move across a particular garden, whether your reception venue will need supplemental lighting after sunset.
Our packages include professional coverage from getting-ready through the reception, expert editing with natural color grading that complements Colorado&#x27;s particular light quality, and a private online gallery with unlimited downloads and a full print release delivered within four weeks. For ceremonies with more than 150 guests, or for couples using expansive venues where simultaneous moments are guaranteed to overlap, we bring dual-photographer coverage so that nothing is missed when the groom sees the bride coming down the aisle while the maid of honor tears up in the front row.
Your finished gallery will include between 400 and 800 carefully edited images — enough to tell the full story of your day without padding it with repetitive frames. The private gallery makes it easy to share with family across Colorado and beyond, and the print release means you are never locked into ordering through a single lab. We want your wedding photographs to live on your walls, in albums, and on the phones of everyone who loved being there.

Fort Collins Wedding Photographer FAQ
How many hours of wedding photography coverage do you recommend for a Fort Collins wedding? 
Most Fort Collins weddings run smoothly with eight to ten hours of coverage, which gives us time to document getting-ready details at your hotel or home, travel to your ceremony site — whether that is a garden venue west of Old Town or a riverside location along the Poudre — and stay through the first hour of open dancing at your reception. If your day includes multiple locations, a lengthy portrait session at Horsetooth Reservoir, or a late-night send-off, we can discuss extended coverage to make sure nothing falls outside the window.
What is included in your Fort Collins wedding photography packages? 
Every package includes a pre-wedding consultation to build your personalized shot list, full professional coverage on your wedding day, expert editing with delivery of 400 to 800 images, a private online gallery with unlimited downloads, and a print release so you can order prints from any lab you choose. Packages with dual-photographer coverage are available for larger ceremonies or venues where simultaneous moments require more than one set of eyes.
How many edited photos will we receive from our Fort Collins wedding? 
You can expect between 400 and 800 professionally edited images delivered within four weeks of your wedding day. The exact number depends on the length of your coverage and how many distinct moments your day includes — a ten-hour wedding with dual photographers at a large venue will naturally yield more images than an intimate four-hour elopement at Library Park. Every image in your gallery is fully edited; we do not deliver unculled contact sheets or unedited files.
Do you offer a second photographer for Fort Collins weddings? 
Yes. For ceremonies with more than 150 guests, or for couples whose venue or timeline makes it logistically impossible for a single photographer to be in two critical places at once, we offer dual-photographer coverage. Fort Collins venues like those with separate ceremony gardens and reception halls can create exactly these situations, and having a second photographer means the emotional reactions in the crowd are captured at the same moment as the couple at the altar.
Are there Fort Collins venues or outdoor locations that work especially well for portraits? 
Fort Collins gives us wonderful options depending on the season and the look you want. The cottonwood groves along the Cache la Poudre River are ideal in late spring and fall for soft, dappled light. The Horsetooth Reservoir area west of town offers dramatic foothills backdrops and tends to be less crowded than mountain locations closer to Denver. Old Town&#x27;s alleyways and brick facades work beautifully for couples who want an urban editorial feel. City Park provides open sky and mature tree canopy for late-afternoon golden-hour sessions. We are happy to recommend specific spots based on your wedding date and the light conditions we can expect.
