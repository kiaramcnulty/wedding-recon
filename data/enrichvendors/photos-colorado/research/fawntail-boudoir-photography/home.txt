What is Boudoir Photography? 
 Boudoir (pronounced boo-dwar ) comes from the French word boudoir , meaning a woman’s private room or dressing space. Today, boudoir photography is known for intimate, elegant portraits that celebrate confidence, femininity, and self-expression. I photograph women from Boulder, Denver, Longmont, Louisville, Aspen, Winter Park, Colorado Springs, Fort Collins, and throughout Colorado.
Your Photoshoot in 3 Simple Steps: 
 Booking a boudoir photography session should feel simple and comfortable. If you are curious but unsure, you are exactly where you need to be! 
Step 1 - Chat & Plan
We’ll go over your questions, talk through what to wear and how to prepare, and discuss any ideas or vision you have for your session. I love creative and unique requests, so feel free to share what excites you.
 I’ll also walk you through my best tips and guidance (I have a lot!), then we’ll choose a date for your session.
Step 2 - The Session
Relax and enjoy fully guided posing, Professional hair & makeup (optional!), and thoughtful attention to every detail. 
 I’ll guide you through exactly what to do, where to look, and how to pose, and we’ll chat the whole time.
Step 3 - Your Images 
Receive your images, beautiful, and timeless keepsakes. Images are delivered in a private, password secured gallery. 
 Once you’ve had a chance to enjoy your photos, we’ll talk about designing your book or album, or ordering prints or wall art if you want them.

Boudoir Photography FAQs
What should I expect during a Boudoir Photoshoot? 
 Boudoir sessions are relaxed, guided, and surprisingly fun. I’ll help you with posing, expressions, and movement so you never feel unsure of what to do. Most clients say they feel comfortable within the first few minutes.
Will my photos be private?
 Absolutely. Your privacy is always respected. Images are only shared with permission, and many clients choose to keep their photos completely private.
Do I need modeling experience?
 Not at all. Almost everyone I photograph says they’re not photogenic or don’t know how to pose. That’s completely normal. My job is to guide you through the entire experience so you feel confident and look amazing.
When should I book my photoshoot?
 Most clients book their session 4–8 weeks in advance , especially if they are planning a gift or special occasion.
 But if you have less time than that, reach out anyway! I do last minute sessions all the time. And if you want to book out several months in advance, you’re free to do that too. 
Where do boudoir sessions take place?
 Sessions take place in my Boulder studio, a small guest house type space designed for beautiful natural light and a comfortable, private atmosphere. 
What should I bring to my boudoir session?
 Most clients bring 2–4 outfits they feel good in. This can include lingerie, cozy pieces like a sweater or button-down, or something more personal. 
 You don’t need a lot, simple options that fit well and feel comfortable usually photograph beautifully. we’ll cover this extensively during our pre-session chat, and I’m always happy to help you choose what works best once you arrive.
