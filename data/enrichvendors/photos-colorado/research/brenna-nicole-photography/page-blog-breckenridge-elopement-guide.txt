Breckenridge Elopement Guide
If you are looking for advice on how to have a Breckenridge elopement you’ve come to the right place! This is a comprehensive guide on everything you need to know about how to have a successful elopement experience. With my expert knowledge on how to plan and execute Breckenridge elopements – I am so excited to share my favorite locations, vendors, accommodations, venues, and more!
Why Should You have a Breckenridge Elopement
Breckenridge is home to a variety of scenic destinations such as, waterfalls, rivers, mountains and more. With this much variety in one area it is sure to make your elopement day amazing. If you are an outdoor enthusiast there is no shortage of activities you can participate in too. I highly recommend including some type of outdoor activity as apart of your elopement so that you can really immerse yourself in the environment you are choosing to say, “I do” in.
With Breckenridge being one of the most popular destinations in Colorado it has major grocery stores, restaurants and accommodations that will make your stay comfortable. If you don’t want to be super remote during your elopement, Breckenridge will give you that mountain town feel with city amenities.
Breckenridge is also conveniently located only 2 hours from Denver International Airport. Traveling to Breckenridge is easy, you’ll take major highways to get there and there is a large selection of rental cars at the airport. With Breckenridge being so close to Denver you’ll also have a wider variety of vendors to choose from too.
How to Find Your Perfect Breckenridge Elopement Location 
Finding the right elopement location is one of the most important decisions you’ll make when planning your wedding day. Breckenridge offers a wide variety of landscapes, including towering mountain peaks, tranquil lakes, dense evergreen forests, and charming historic spots within the town itself. The best location for you will depend on your vision for the day, accessibility needs, and seasonal considerations.
If you want a dramatic mountain backdrop with easy access, Sapphire Point Overlook is a top choice. Located between Breckenridge and Frisco, this stunning overlook offers panoramic views of Lake Dillon and the Ten-mile Range. The site can be reserved for private ceremonies, ensuring a level of privacy that many couples desire.
Lake Dillon as mentioned above can also be used as an elopement location. There are many spots along the lakeshore that have stunning views that would offer an amazing backdrop to your elopement.
For couples who love adventure, Boreas Pass is an excellent option. This high-elevation mountain pass features breathtaking views and is especially beautiful in the fall when the aspen trees turn a vibrant golden yellow. The pass is closed to vehicles in the winter, but it remains accessible for snowshoeing or cross-country skiing.
Located about 45 minutes from Breckenridge, Loveland Pass sits at nearly 12,000 feet and offers panoramic views of the Continental Divide. This location is ideal for couples looking for a dramatic mountain backdrop without requiring a long hike. It’s accessible year-round, though winter elopements may require warm clothing due to strong winds and cold temperatures.
Just a short drive south of Breckenridge, Hoosier Pass is another high-elevation location with breathtaking views of the Mosquito Range and the Tenmile Range. This spot is perfect for couples who want a secluded setting with an easily accessible pull-off from the highway. Fall is particularly stunning here, with golden aspens framing the mountains.
For an adventurous elopement with a longer hike, Mohawk Lakes is a top choice. This moderate-to-challenging trail leads to stunning alpine lakes and waterfalls, surrounded by rugged mountain peaks. The 6.6-mile round-trip hike rewards couples with a breathtakingly scenic and private ceremony location.
Benefits of Eloping in Breckenridge 
Eloping in Breckenridge allows couples to experience the best of Colorado’s natural beauty without the stress of planning a large, traditional wedding. One of the biggest advantages of choosing this mountain town is its accessibility. Unlike many remote elopement destinations, Breckenridge is just a two-hour drive from Denver International Airport, making it easy for couples and guests to travel there.
Another benefit is the variety of seasonal experiences available. Whether you’re dreaming of a snowy winter elopement, a wildflower-filled summer ceremony, or a fall wedding among golden aspens, Breckenridge offers year-round beauty. Additionally, elopements in this area can be as adventurous or as relaxed as you want. Couples can incorporate skiing, hiking, or even a hot air balloon ride into their wedding day, creating unforgettable memories in one of the most scenic locations in the country.
Breckenridge also offers the rare opportunity to self-solemnize. In Colorado, couples can legally marry themselves without an officiant or witnesses, making the ceremony incredibly personal and meaningful.
How to Plan a Breckenridge Elopement 
Planning a successful Breckenridge elopement starts with setting a date and deciding on the type of scenery you envision for your ceremony. The time of year will influence location accessibility, weather conditions, and the activities available for your celebration.
Once you’ve chosen a date, researching permit requirements is crucial. Many locations in the White River National Forest and other public lands require a permit for wedding ceremonies. It’s best to check with the U.S. Forest Service or the local county office well in advance.
Choosing the right vendors can also make a significant difference in your experience. Hiring a local elopement photographer ensures that you’ll have someone familiar with the best locations and lighting conditions. If you want professional floral arrangements, hair and makeup, or catering for a small reception, working with local vendors who understand the mountain environment can simplify your planning process.
Since Breckenridge sits at an elevation of over 9,600 feet, preparing for the altitude is essential. Couples and guests who aren’t used to high elevations should arrive a few days early to acclimate, stay hydrated, and avoid excessive alcohol consumption.
Best Time of Year to Have a Breckenridge Elopement 
Each season in Breckenridge offers a unique atmosphere and challenges for elopements. Winter is ideal for couples who love snow and want a magical winter wonderland setting. However, cold temperatures and unpredictable weather mean that warm attire and backup plans for indoor locations may be necessary.
Spring in Breckenridge can be unpredictable, with snow lingering into May and trails often being muddy due to snowmelt. However, this season offers fewer crowds and the chance to experience the transition from winter to summer.
Summer provides the most accessible conditions for elopements, with long days, warm temperatures, and vibrant wildflowers covering the mountainsides. This is the best time for high-elevation locations like Boreas Pass, as all roads and trails are open.
Fall is one of the most breathtaking times for a Breckenridge elopement. The golden aspens create a stunning contrast against the evergreen forests and blue skies. The weather is typically mild, but snow can arrive as early as October.
Activities to Do for Your Breckenridge Elopement 
Incorporating activities into your elopement day can make it even more special. Many couples choose to take a sunrise or sunset hike to capture incredible photos in the best lighting conditions. If you’re visiting in the winter, skiing or snowboarding together on Breckenridge’s world-famous slopes can be a fun and memorable way to celebrate.
Other options include booking a private sleigh ride, soaking in hot springs nearby, taking a scenic gondola ride, or even going dog sledding. In the summer, couples can enjoy paddleboarding on Lake Dillon, mountain biking, or exploring Breckenridge’s historic downtown.
Marriage and Permitting Laws for a Breckenridge Elopement 
One of the best aspects of eloping in Colorado is the ability to self-solemnize. This means that you and your partner can legally marry yourselves without an officiant or witnesses. To obtain a marriage license, couples must visit the Summit County Clerk’s Office in Breckenridge. The license is valid immediately and costs around $30.
Certain public lands require a permit for wedding ceremonies, so it’s important to research your chosen location in advance. The White River National Forest, for example, has regulations on group sizes and environmental impact.
Traveling to Breckenridge 
The most convenient way to reach Breckenridge is by flying into Denver International Airport and renting a car or taking a shuttle to town. During winter months, road conditions can be challenging, so renting a vehicle with all-wheel drive is recommended.
Best Airbnbs and Restaurants for Your Breckenridge Elopement 
For accommodations, cozy mountain cabins, ski chalets, and luxurious lodges are available throughout Breckenridge. Staying near Main Street provides easy access to restaurants and shops, while more remote rentals offer seclusion and mountain views.
After your elopement, celebrating with a meal at one of Breckenridge’s top restaurants can be the perfect way to end the day. Hearthstone Restaurant, known for its fine dining and historic charm, is a popular choice, while Breckenridge Brewery offers a more casual atmosphere with craft beers and comfort food.
Final Thoughts on Planning a Breckenridge Elopement 
Eloping in Breckenridge allows couples to celebrate their love in a stunning and meaningful way. Whether you choose a quiet alpine lake, a dramatic mountain overlook, or a cozy downtown venue, Breckenridge offers unforgettable locations and experiences for your wedding day. By planning ahead, securing the right permits, and embracing the adventure, you can create a truly magical elopement experience in the heart of the Rockies.
