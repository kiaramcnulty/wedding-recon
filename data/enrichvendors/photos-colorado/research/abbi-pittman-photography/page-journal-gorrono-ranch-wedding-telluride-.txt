A Gorrono Ranch Wedding in Telluride, Colorado | Matt & Caitrin
August in Telluride has a way of slowing everything down; the light lingers, the mountains feel closer, and moments unfold without needing to be rushed. Matt and Caitrin’s Gorrono Ranch wedding was a perfect reflection of that rhythm: intentional, relaxed, and deeply connected.
 The day began with a quiet, rainy morning in Mountain Village. Caitrin got ready surrounded by close friends and family, with soft mountain light and an easy calm filling the room. Matt spent the morning nearby playing cards with his closest friends. 
 They chose to do a first look, tucked into the landscape just beyond the lodge. With the mountains as their backdrop, it felt private and grounding, a chance to connect before the day unfolded. Their reactions were natural and unfiltered, exactly the kind of moments documentary photography is built around.
 The ceremony took place on the deck overlooking the Wilson Peak range. With the mountains standing tall behind them, Matt and Caitrin exchanged vows in a way that felt effortless and sincere. Nothing was forced, just presence, emotion, and a setting that didn’t need embellishment.
 As the sun dipped lower, we slipped away for sunset photos. The August light was soft and golden, giving space for quiet moments and movement without direction. These portraits felt honest and timeless, Matt and Caitrin simply being themselves in a place they love.
 This Gorrono Ranch wedding was a reminder that the most meaningful days don’t need excess just intention, connection, and room for real moments. For couples looking for Telluride wedding inspo, this day was quietly unforgettable.
