# Type rules — music acts (`entries` blocks label: MUSIC ACT)

## Wrong type? Two tiers (draft-contract has the full rule; write NO rows for either)
- `NOTMUSIC!:<slug>` (STRONG — positive evidence, auto-removed) — the dossier shows a
  venue that hosts live music (but isn't itself a bookable act), an AV/production rental
  company, a music school or instrument shop, or a photo/video vendor.
- `NOTMUSIC:<slug>` (SOFT — looks off but not certain) — draft the row(s) normally and
  flag; the orchestrator vets before removing.
- KEEP (never a flag): a DJ company that also rents uplighting/photo booths IS a music act.
  An entertainment AGENCY booking multiple acts is also in scope — keep, and say it's an
  agency.

## service_region — REQUIRED on every row
- Where they perform, sourced from the dossier. **Multi-state service is common for this
  type — look for it and state it exactly** ("Colorado + Wyoming", "CO/UT/NM", "Front
  Range + destination"). Narrowest sourced wins; a base city with stated travel gets the
  travel scope. Nothing narrower sourced → the run's state. Never invent; never blank
  (upload hard-fails).

## What couples actually want captured
- WHAT they are, first: DJ vs live band vs pianist vs singer vs string quartet vs agency.
- What they cover: ceremony, cocktail hour, reception, late-night sets/EDM, dinner music.
- Music style: genres (Motown, bluegrass, top-40), instruments, whether they take
  requests or learn a first-dance song.
- **"See them live":** free showcases, public gigs, venue residencies where a couple can
  check them out before booking — capture specifics when sourced (this is gold for this
  type).
- Firsthand color from reviews/reddit, attributed: energy, dance floor, MC quality,
  volume complaints, reliability.

## price_details specifics
- Piece-count tiers ("5-piece band from $X, 7-piece from $Y"), hourly/package rates,
  ceremony-only vs full-day pricing, add-ons (uplighting, MC services, extra hours,
  photo booth, learning a song), deposits/retainers. Travel fees ONLY if a source states
  them — if unclear, don't mention travel at all.
